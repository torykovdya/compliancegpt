ACME CORPORATION — INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Effective Date: March 1, 2026
Owner: Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

1. PASSWORD POLICY
- Minimum length: 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, and special character
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Reuse of the last 5 passwords is prohibited
- Default passwords must be changed within 24 hours of account creation
- Password sharing between employees is strictly prohibited

2. ACCESS CONTROL
- Principle of least privilege: employees receive only the access required for their role
- Access reviews are conducted quarterly by department heads
- Admin privileges require CISO approval and are reviewed every 30 days
- Former employees' access is revoked within 2 hours of termination
- Shared accounts are prohibited; each employee must have a unique account

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION
- Level 1 (Public): Marketing materials, press releases — no restrictions
- Level 2 (Internal): Internal memos, team calendars — employees only
- Level 3 (Confidential): Customer data, financial reports, HR records — need-to-know basis, encrypted
- Level 4 (Restricted): M&A documents, source code, encryption keys — executive access only, air-gapped storage

4. INCIDENT REPORTING
- All security incidents must be reported within 1 hour of discovery
- Report to: security@acme.internal or via the Incident Response Portal
- Do NOT attempt to investigate or remediate independently — contact the Security Team
- Preserve all evidence; do not delete or modify affected systems
- Ransomware: disconnect from network immediately, do NOT pay the ransom

5. THIRD-PARTY VENDORS
- All vendors accessing company data must sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Vendor security assessments are conducted annually
- Vendors with access to Level 3+ data must undergo additional security audits
- Subcontracting without written consent is prohibited

6. AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE
- Annual security audit by an independent third party
- Quarterly internal audits by the Security Team
- All audit findings must be addressed within 30 days
- Non-compliance may result in regulatory fines and reputational damage

7. ACCEPTABLE USE
- Company devices and network are for business purposes primarily
- Personal use must not interfere with work productivity
- Installing unauthorized software is prohibited
- All internet activity is logged and may be monitored for security purposes
- Social media use during work hours must comply with the Social Media Policy

For questions, contact: security@acme.internal
